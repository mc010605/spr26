Lodge Dutch Oven

By: Matthew Chen  |  Overall Rating: 5/5  |  Age: 1 year of use  |  Date: March 4, 2026

Pros & Cons

  • Pros: Stains Easily Removed, Large Size, Great Heat Conductivity, BPA Free, Oven Safe
  • Cons: Moderately Heavy, Fragile, Large Size

Overview

The Lodge Dutch Oven is an extremely useful piece of cookware that I think should be a part of anyone's arsenal when trying to lose weight. It is highly durable, versatile, and well designed. It's made from porcelain-enameled cast iron. You can use it for slow braising, cooking bread, and searing. The company Lodge has a good reputation of being affordable and high quality.

I personally love using the Lodge Dutch Oven to prepare large stews, where I slow braise meat so that leaner cuts become tender. Cooking this way also prevents the meat from being dry because I am cooking it within a broth. This allows me to make 4-5 days of food in a single sitting and therefore it allows me to meal-prep for the week and stay on my calorie goals. This way, I can never be too lazy to cook, since all I have to do is heat it up, whether on the stove or in the microwave.

This scale is everything to me. There are many nuances when it comes to weighing out your food. Therefore, it is very important to weigh out everything you eat. For example, when I do make my stews in the Lodge Dutch Oven, I like to keep a note of how much of each ingredient (in grams) I am putting into the stew.

Nowadays, AI can even help with your nutrient tracking. For example, after compiling the list of ingredients and how much is in my stew, I like to input it into Gemini to create an artificial 100g label of the stew I made. That way, when I am tracking my calories, I simply use the label as a reference, weigh out how much of the stew I am eating using the food scale, and then I track my calories that way. It is extremely precise, but also very important for staying on track for me!

Build Quality & Design

The Lodge Dutch Oven is designed in a way that allows great heat conductivity and distribution. This greatly improves cooking efficiency and results. The large size allows you to cook a lot of food without worrying about overflowing. For example, I can cook up to 3.5 lbs of meat at once alongside veggies and broth. Now when I say 3.5 lbs, I'm referring to uncooked meats. The article linked talks more about the difference between weighing cooked and uncooked meat. The material is BPA free and does not leech any toxic chemicals into your food even if there are scratches. The enamel on the cast iron makes it so that you don't have to season the pot like you usually do for traditional cast iron tools. It also prevents rust, making it so that you can air dry your dutch oven without any concerns. The loop handles are big enough to allow you to use oven mitts. The lid is also really well made so that it traps moisture inside the dutch oven well — this matters a lot for slow-braising stews and meats. The primary drawback is that it is pretty heavy, around 5–7 lbs. However, cleaning stains off the bottom is straightforward with some steel wool. Another drawback to consider is that it is relatively fragile.

Importance to Losing Weight

The reason why this dutch oven is extremely important to weight loss is because having food prepared and ready to eat is very important for staying on track on a diet. There's no opportunity to "feel too lazy" to cook since it's a simple matter of microwaving some of the stew. Additionally, making tender meat from stews is a matter of simmering for a few hours — it's low-effort. The stews are also extremely nourishing, so there's no need to eat bland food when losing weight.

Taylor Digital Kitchen Scale

By: Matthew Chen  |  Overall Rating: 4/5  |  Age: 1 year of use  |  Date: March 5, 2026

Pros & Cons

  • Pros: Accurate measurements, Tare function, Compact and lightweight, Affordable, Multiple unit conversions (g, oz, lb)
  • Cons: Limited Capacity, Not waterproof, Cannot fit large bowls

Overview

The Taylor Digital Kitchen Scale is known for its simplicity and reliability. This scale is commonly used for cooking, baking, and especially for tracking food intake. In the context of weight loss, I weigh nearly everything with this thing. Precision matters when it comes to weight loss — guesstimating too many times can lead to accumulated calories that go untracked. This scale is very beginner friendly with only 2 buttons and a really simple design.

Build Quality & Design

This scale has a smooth surface that is easy to clean. Since it is very lightweight, it's practical for everyday use. The low-profile design allows it to be easily stored in a drawer or left on the countertop. The front-facing digital display is clear and easy to read even in normal lighting. Two simple buttons — one for unit conversion and one for tare — keep the interface intuitive. Where the Taylor scale truly shines is in its accuracy. It provides consistent measurements in grams, ounces, and other units. The tare function allows you to subtract container weight, which is really important for precision. The response time is quick, and readings stabilize within a second or two. While it may not be lab-grade, it performs reliably for all typical kitchen needs. Cleaning is easy due to the flat surface. However, since it is not waterproof, you should avoid excessive moisture. Battery life is solid, though the auto shut-off can occasionally trigger too quickly during longer prep sessions.

Importance to Losing Weight

A food scale is one of the most effective tools for weight loss because it eliminates guesswork. I personally weigh every single bit of food I eat. Estimating portion sizes often leads to underestimating calorie intake, which can stall progress. By weighing food in grams, you can track macros and calories with much higher accuracy. When making large recipes, I weigh everything in grams and insert the recipe into an AI assistant to get a per-100g label. When it comes time to eat, I simply weigh my portion and use that label to track nutrition accurately.
