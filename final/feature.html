Now I won’t bore you with a regular day-in-the-life. In fact, I think that it’s probably far more effective for me to give you a very brief timeline of what a typical/ideal day looks like for me as someone that’s trying to lose body fat and retain my muscle mass.

The Sleep

I discuss the importance of sleep on the other parts of my webpage, but specifically, I’d like to highlight something here. I’ve always been someone who has had trouble keeping a consistent schedule. Whether that be because I have social events, classes or exams, there's always a reason why my schedule gets messed up. The reason why this is important is because sleeping and waking up at the same time every single day is one of the best ways to get some of the best quality sleep you’ll ever have.

A lot of people, especially those in university, have trouble keeping a consistent sleep schedule. And I understand that as I struggle with that too. But I believe that simply being aware and trying your best to maintain some consistency is a good way to improve your weight loss journey.

Now this matters not only because sleep is how your body recovers, but also it gives you the proper rest necessary for you to have sufficient energy for the next day. If you want to learn more about this, then definitely check out this article. To keep things brief, your circadian rhythm dictates how your body takes you between the different phases of sleep. In the REM phase, or deep sleep phase, that’s where your body does the most recovery. Making your circadian rhythm normal is the best way to reach REM sleep consistently and for enough time each night.

Timing Your Meals

This applies for each of my meals throughout the day. Timing when you eat is extremely important not only to curb your hunger and your cravings, but it’s also necessary in order to keep your body functioning as you provide it with the energy it needs, when it needs it, throughout the day.

Before my workout, I like to have a really carb-heavy breakfast. Specifically, I like my oatmeal with my choice of fruit. The reason why you want to do this is because carbohydrates are your body’s main source of energy when weightlifting or doing cardio. You want to make sure that the quality of your workouts and cardio is as high as possible. Without it, your body’s muscles won’t be able to perform the way it’s usually able to. As a result, you will end up losing muscle as you lose weight which is something you want to avoid at all costs.

For lunch, I like to have a super protein-heavy meal with a considerable amount of carbs. After working out and depleting your body and your muscles of all the energy you have, you absolutely have to refuel in order to ensure that your body has enough energy for the rest of the day. That’s the reason why you need to still have some carbohydrates after your workout. The reason why you need to have protein is because this is what some might call the “anabolic window” where nutrient intake is most crucial for muscle repair. After all, lifting weights just created a ton of microtears within your muscles which now need repairing. By eating protein right after your workout, you’ll be able to maximize the amount of recovery that your muscles can have. This also matters a lot because recovering your muscles so that they aren’t sore for the next time that you work them out is integral for constantly improving your strength and making sure you have good workouts.

For dinner, I tend to keep myself busy whether it's classes or homework, until later at night. This is because, on a calorie deficit while you’re trying to lose weight, it will be extremely easy for you to overeat. By purposely eating late, you’ll prevent yourself from going to bed hungry, which is really important so that you don’t end up having that late-night snack that ends up ruining your calorie goals for the day. However, you do have to consider your digestive system. It’s also important that you don’t eat right before bed as that has been linked to fat gain and a poor digestion throughout the night.

The Workout

If you looked at the timeline closely, you’ll see that I allotted 2 hours of time for my workout and my cardio. Now, based on your goals, this is not strictly necessary. The reason why I spend so much time in the gym is because I am currently spending 1 whole hour on the treadmill to get through this point in time where I am at a plateau. However, based on your strength goals, you can reduce the amount of time you lift weights. Based on your fat loss goals, you can also reduce the amount of cardio you do in a given day. Either way, the question is: how do you manage to make the time for working out and cardio? Personally, I have had days where I have lifted weights, gone to class, finished my work, and came back to the gym to do my cardio. Other times, I’ll tactically remove a set or two to save myself some time so that I can fit in my workout. It doesn’t have to be an exact science and it’s okay to give yourself some leeway sometimes. I am a personal believer that consistency is the foundation of any fat loss goal. Though you might have 1 day a week where you struggle, showing up and meeting your goals the other 6 days will eventually give you progress. Obviously, if that number exceeds 1, then you’ll have to consider how you can change your lifestyle to keep that number to 1.

These are just a few niche things that I do to stay consistent with my goals. Reach out to me if you have any questions about anything else that I might have missed!
